§2How this logbook works

This logbook is the record of your clinical immersion — the part of this rotation that no online course can replicate. For each of the eight entrustable professional activities (EPAs) below, log the encounters where you observed, participated in, or performed the activity, and have your supervisor confirm the level you reached.

You do not need to log every patient. Log representative encounters that show your progression toward each EPA's target level. Aim for quality of evidence over quantity. Six rows are provided per EPA; print additional pages if you need more.

The entrustability scale

1
Observed
Watched the activity performed by a clinician.
2
Assisted
Participated under direct, hands-on guidance.
3
Supervised
Performed with a supervisor present or immediately available.
4
Independent
Performed competently without direct supervision, within scope.

Each EPA has a realistic target level for a six-week rotation — some activities (history-taking, interpreting results) can reach "supervised," while others (oocyte retrieval, ultrasound) are observation- or assistance-level for a student. Targets are shown on each EPA and are honest by design; a logbook that claimed independence in egg collection after six weeks would not survive accreditation review.
